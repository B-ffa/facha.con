On staying interested

I keep noticing that most worthwhile work begins before confidence arrives. Interest comes first, then attention, then skill. I used to think seriousness was a precondition for good work, but I’m starting to think affection is. You return to what you like long enough to become good at it.

March 28, 2026

A note on repetition

Repetition has a bad reputation because people confuse it with stagnation. But nearly everything useful is built by returning to the same subject with slightly better taste each time. The outside world sees sameness; the person doing the work feels the difference immediately.

Why I write things down

Writing is less a performance than a method of finding out what I think. A vague intuition can feel complete in the mind right up until it meets a sentence. The sentence is the test. If it collapses, the thought was never finished.
